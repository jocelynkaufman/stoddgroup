Before You List

Should You Sell?

Selling isn't always the right move. Sometimes the smartest play is keeping the property and letting it work for you in a different way. Here are two alternatives we'll walk through with you before you list — with honest math on each, no pressure either direction.

🏡

Rent It Out

Most homeowners can actually afford to buy a new home and keep their current one as a rental — especially if you've been in the home a few years. Today's Utah rents typically cover the mortgage on a long-held home with room left over for cash flow.

✓Hold onto Utah's appreciation curve instead of cashing out
✓Add a passive income stream that grows with rent inflation
✓Tenants pay down your mortgage while you build the next portfolio
✓Unlock tax benefits you don't get on a primary residence

We'll run the numbers with you — current cash flow potential, refinance options to fund your next purchase, and a realistic 5- and 10-year projection so you can compare it against selling outright.

Explore Renting It Out
🔄

1031 Exchange

If the property is (or has been) an investment, a 1031 exchange lets you sell and roll the entire proceeds into a "like-kind" replacement property — without writing the IRS a check on the gain. Trade up, change markets, improve cash flow.

✓Defer all federal capital gains tax on the sale
✓Trade into a bigger asset, better-cash-flowing property, or new market
✓Reset depreciation on the replacement property
✓Keep stacking until you pass it on at stepped-up basis

We'll connect you with qualified intermediaries, walk you through the 45-day identification and 180-day closing windows, and help you decide whether a 1031 actually fits your goals or if a straight sale makes more sense.

Timing Matters

When Should You Sell?

Utah's seasonal rhythm affects days on market, competition, and ultimately your sale price. Here's how each season tends to play out.

🌸

Spring

MARCH – MAY

Peak market energy. The most buyers, the highest competition among listings.

Best for: sellers who want top dollar quickly.
Watch for: inventory floods the market — pricing strategy matters more than ever.

☀️

Summer

JUNE – AUGUST

Strong and steady. Families want to move before school starts.

Best for: family-friendly neighborhoods.
Watch for: slight cooldown after July 4th.

🍂

Fall

SEPTEMBER – NOVEMBER

Serious buyers only. Less foot traffic, but fewer tire-kickers.

Best for: sellers trading up into their next home.
Watch for: rate volatility as the year winds down.

❄️

Winter

DECEMBER – FEBRUARY

Slowest months. Low inventory means your listing stands out.

Best for: urgency sellers and unique properties.
Watch for: weather delays and slower closings.

Beyond the season: falling interest rates drive buyer surges regardless of month. Life events matter more than timing — if you need to sell now, you sell now. And don't forget the two-year primary-residence rule for the capital-gains exclusion.
