What is a 1031 Exchange?

A 1031 exchange (named after Section 1031 of the IRS tax code) allows you to defer paying capital gains taxes when you sell an investment property by reinvesting the proceeds into a "like-kind" property. This powerful strategy lets you keep more money working for you instead of paying it to the IRS.

💰

Defer Capital Gains

Avoid paying 15-20% capital gains tax by rolling proceeds into your next investment property.

📈

Grow Your Portfolio

Keep 100% of your equity working for you to acquire larger or better-performing properties.

🏢

Upgrade Properties

Trade up from smaller properties to larger multi-units or better locations without tax penalties.

How the 1031 Exchange Process Works

1

Sell Your Property

List and sell your investment property. Proceeds go to a qualified intermediary, not to you.

2

45-Day Identification

Within 45 days of closing, identify up to 3 replacement properties in writing.

3

180-Day Close

Close on your replacement property within 180 days of selling the original property.

4

Defer Taxes

Complete the exchange and defer all capital gains taxes to your next sale.

Important: Strict IRS deadlines and rules apply. Working with an experienced agent and qualified intermediary is essential for a successful 1031 exchange.
